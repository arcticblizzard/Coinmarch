Campaign Frame

Coinmarch Campaign

A struggle for control of the Pillars of Destinies, where prophecy and revolution collide.

The Pitch

“Long ago, twelve Pillars stood as linchpins between worlds… now they awaken, and you must choose which destiny to forge.”

Tone & Feel

  • Mythic
  • Revolutionary
  • Grim-hope

Themes

  • Power and Prophecy
  • Betrayal & Redemption
  • Civil Unrest

Touchstones

  • *Game of Thrones* meets *Foundation*
  • *Mistborn* trilogies
  • *Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild*

Overview

The Twelve Pillars loom across this world—ancient monoliths that shape fate. When they stir, the delicate balance fractures, and every power will vie for control…

Communities

  • The Pillarbound: Guardians sworn to protect a single Pillar. S0 Question: Which oath binds you most—blood or duty?
  • The Free Cities: Merchant-states built on prophecy trade. S0 Question: What secret might you haggle away?
  • Nomadic Diviners: Oracles who chase the Pillars’ voices. S0 Question: Whose future haunts your visions?

Ancestries

  • Human • Aelfkin • Stoneborn • Sky-touched • Wildbore

Classes

  • Scepterbearer • Pillarbinder • Seer • Revolutionist • …

Player Principles

  • Prophecy First: Everyone has a stake in the vision.
  • Epic Risks: If you’re not risking your Pillar’s favor, you’re playing too safe.
  • Revolution & Trust: Alliances are temporary—betray wisely.

GM Principles

  • Prophecy as Compass: Use visions to guide, not dictate.
  • Balance Factions: Every power should feel both dangerous and sympathetic.
  • Hard Choices: True stakes demand real consequences.

Distinctions

Use Pillar events as your scene-framing device; each session opens with a Pillar’s omen.

Inciting Incident

The Pillar of Dawn flares to life overnight—bathing the capital in golden light and awakening half-remembered prophecies.

Campaign Mechanics

  • Prophecy Tokens: Spend to claim minor fated boons.
  • Pillar Corruption: A rising tide mechanic that alters scenes.

Session Zero Questions

  • Which Pillar did your family once serve—and why did you abandon it?
  • What prophecy have you heard whispered at your birth?
  • Who in the group is closest to revolution, and who clings to order?
